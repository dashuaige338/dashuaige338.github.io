The Atlas of Human Germline Transposable Elements

This germline TEdb was created based on published bulk RNA-seq datasets and single cell RNA-seq datasets (10x Genomics) with the purpose of enabling a broad audience to explore transposable element (TE) and gene expression profiles from undifferentiated human embryonic stem cells (hESCs), differentiated cells and human Primordial Germ Cell Like Cells (hPGCLCs). Currently under revision in Nature Communications.

Transcription Factors - Transposable Element network

TF-TE network is build according to ChIP-Seq data mainly from ENCODE database, mainly developed by Mr Ziwei Xue. We focus on binding of transcription factors to knowned transposable element regions, and analysis the significance of the interaction. Our goal is to predict interaction between TFs and TEs and their functions in embryogenesis and cancer progression. Currently wet labs for some TF-TE interactions identified by our database are going.
